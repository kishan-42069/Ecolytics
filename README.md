# 🌿 Ecolytics — Carbon Footprint Tracker

Ecolytics is a beautiful, 100% private, and science-backed personal carbon footprint tracker and simulator. It makes your invisible climate footprint visible and helps you take meaningful action to reduce it.

---

## ✨ Features

- **📊 Carbon Dashboard**: Get a real-time snapshot of your monthly carbon footprint across key categories (Transport, Home Energy, Food, and Shopping) compared with regional averages.
- **📝 Activity Logger**: Easily log your monthly habits—miles driven, transit taken, energy consumed, and meals eaten—to see your impact score update instantly.
- **🔄 "What If?" Simulator**: Drag interactive sliders to model lifestyle changes (e.g., driving 20% less, switching to green energy) and see exactly how much CO₂ you could save.
- **🤖 Ask Eco (AI Coach)**: Chat with a built-in AI assistant for personalized, jargon-free advice on starting your sustainability journey.
- **🏅 Goals & Achievements**: Stay motivated by maintaining a green streak, completing eco challenges, earning level-up XP, and unlocking achievements.
- **📸 Instagram Story Export**: Generate custom 9:16 aspect ratio reports of your carbon footprint to share with your friends and inspire action.
- **🔒 100% Private & Local**: No account, no servers, and no tracking. All data is saved entirely in your browser's local storage.

---

## 🚀 How to Run Locally

Since Ecolytics is built with vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, you can run it directly in your browser without any build tools.

### Option 1: Live Server (Recommended)
If you are using VS Code:
1. Install the **Live Server** extension.
2. Click **Go Live** in the status bar at the bottom right.
3. Your browser will automatically open the project at `http://127.0.0.1:5500/`.

### Option 2: Double-click
Simply double-click the `index.html` file in your file explorer to open it in your browser.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Science

- **Frontend**: Vanilla HTML5, Custom CSS3, Modern JavaScript (ES6)
- **Data & Averages**: Carbon calculations are powered by regional emission factors from peer-reviewed databases and organizations including the **IPCC**, **US EPA**, and **MoEFCC (India)**.
